Inspirational Articles

The Story Behind the Worst Movie in History

Basically, this article says a decent Bollywood movie (Gunday) became the lowest rank imdb film bc it offended a group of Bangladeshi supporters in the way it described the India-Pakistan conflict of the 70s. Cue online protests rating the movie poorly. Thesis: without context, data can be misleading.

https://fivethirtyeight.com/features/the-story-behind-the-worst-movie-on-imdb/

What If Online Movie Ratings Weren’t Only Based on What Men Think?

Men are vastly more represented in imdb ratings, the lists reflect their preferences. The author debates whether re-weighting is a solution, and finds that the top 100 still come from the top 250, not some kind of dramatic manufactured change, recommends imdb does this.

https://fivethirtyeight.com/features/what-if-online-movie-ratings-werent-based-almost-entirely-on-what-men-think/

Be Suspicious of Online Movie Ratings, Especially Fandango’s

No movie gets fewer than 3 stars, Fandango is owned by NBC and has interest in you going to theaters; inflates reviews. Has a data set attached that gives comprehensive (but old) rotten tomatoes & fandango review data. Last updated 5 years ago.

https://fivethirtyeight.com/features/fandango-movies-ratings/

Whose Ratings Should you Trust?

Evaluates fandango, RT, imdb, metcritic. Says that the best source will be the one whose reviews most resemble a normal/Gaussian distribution. Visualizes the distributions of each: Fandango’s are skewed too positive, tomatometer is flat/no distribution, says metacritic’s score most closely resembles a normal distribution.

https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/whose-reviews-should-you-trust-imdb-rotten-tomatoes-metacritic-or-fandango-7d1010c6cf19/
